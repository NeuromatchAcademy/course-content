<p>The Neuromatch Academy projects are small group projects that will help the students build the skill of taking a question and trying to apply concepts or tools from the NMA curriculum. Mentors are a critical component of this process and you will be providing guidance and support for these projects. The primary goals are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Understanding how to develop a clear scientific question</li>
<li>Understanding the limits of a particular dataset</li>
<li>Understanding how to select the appropriate techniques or tools to answer the scientific question given the available data</li>
<li>Interactive discussions with mentors who can provide experience, guidance and support for scientific growth</li>
<li>Completing a full cycle of the scientific process: question generation, analysis, generation of a presentation of the findings</li>
</ul>
<p>We expect mentors to</p>
<ul>
<li>Help students with assessing feasibility of projects (week 1)</li>
<li>Help students to solidify ideas and questions, by enforcing clarity</li>
<li>Promote course materials and toolkits provided by the NMA</li>
<li>Help students use tools like a (neuro)scientist vs like a data scientist</li>
<li>Provide scientific advice, without getting too involved in their projects</li>
<li>Familiarize themselves with the datasets selected for projects</li>
</ul>
<p>Two important things to remember:</p>
<ul>
<li>The exact question and data set is not important, we are interested in the process</li>
<li>The âqualityâ, ânoveltyâ and âdepthâ of the question and outcome are less important than completing the full cycle from question generation to modeling or analysis to presenting the question and results</li>
</ul>
<p>As a Mentor, you will have two roles:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Week one</strong>: brainstorming and project idea refinement</li>
<li><strong>Week two and three</strong>: guiding projects to completion</li>
</ul>
<p>The NMA students are grouped into Pods, which are run by a TA. The Pods are located in a zone and with a grouping within that zone based on their local timezone. The NMA projects are divided into two components. In week one, you will be assigned to a Pod and will help the students within the Pod refine their project ideas. You should look over the <a href="https://github.com/NeuromatchAcademy/course-content">topics</a> being covered in the summer school (with an <a href="https://youtu.be/T1VSYU-H8-Y" rel="nofollow">introduction video</a>) and try to help them select approaches, concepts and tools to help them answer their scientific question from the curriculum content. We have provided curated datasets and a selection of <a href="https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1WAHfJcBPM4rmwwvreAAS92sRYtltJRwklxH-82NzCYo/edit#slide=id.g8225ef52cb_0_10" rel="nofollow">project ideas</a> provided by the Mentors. By the end of week one, the students should have selected a project that is coherent and can be applied to a dataset (unless purely theoretical/computational) Normally, this should be one of the NMA datasets, but if you feel the students have a clear, just ocoherent plan that is achievable in two weeks working 2h a day, they can use other public datasets.</p>
<p>In weeks two and three, you will be assigned to specific projects, matched to your general area of research. Your goal in weeks two and three is to help the students through the process of analyzing data from datasets. The students value the discussion and interactions with Mentors, we would like you to be open to general discussions about science, but you should also ensure the projects are on track.</p>
